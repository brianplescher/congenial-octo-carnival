Woundwise: Dissolution, Abjective Ecology, and Subversive Becoming

nonfiction · experimental philosophy · 2025

Woundwise examines collapse as a methodology. Through critical writing and field research, this experimental philosophy book explores how wounds—personal, cultural, and social—can be reimagined not as endpoints but as openings for transformation. A deep dive into dissolution philosophy, abjective ecology, and post-structural thought.

About Brian

Brian L. Plescher is a writer and philosopher exploring the thresholds between dissolution, creativity, and renewal. His work engages post-structural thought, ecology, and systems theory to investigate how collapse can become a generative force. Author of Woundwise and contributor of philosophy essays on mutual aid and abjective ecology.

He holds a Master of Science in Leadership, Management, and Strategy from Michigan State University, a Master of Divinity from Austin Presbyterian Theological Seminary, and Executive Education from Yale School of Management.
